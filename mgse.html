Lunar Solar Array

Photo credit: NASA/David DeHoyos

LAMPS (the Lunar Array Mast and Power System) is a Honeybee Robotics (Blue Origin, In-Space Systems) NASA-funded project aimed towards developing a solar array for energy collection on the Moon.


Prior to testing within the historic Chamber A at NASA JSC, a number of ambient and thermal chamber tests of maximal mast deployment with subsystem integration had to be completed.


I designed, developed, and fabricated the test fixture to integrate the deployable 50 ft tall mast, mast storage reel, base electrical box, and electrical umbilical spool.


I also helped assemble/ re-assemble and prepare all of the aluminum bands and doublers for the mast :)

CAD of test stand with all subsystems integrated

Design Considerations

Physical test stand after machining and assembly
  • Total structure height: shorter = greater mast deployment height
  • Total mass & footprint: minimized = easily portable for testing
  • Electrical harnessing constraints and alignment for electrical connectors
  • Stability of structure
  • Minimizing machining due to required turn-around time

Solution

Mast before and after deployment in thermal chamber
  • Design 4" t-slot framing assembly in Autodesk Inventor CAD
  • Add 1" t-slot framing supports where needed
  • Integrate all sub-assemblies and confirm positioning with relevant sub-teams
  • Add removable caster wheels to structure

Deployment Testing

First full-stroke outdoor deployment with the team :)
  • Test stand was used in thermal cycling, ambient mast deployments, and full-stroke deployment tests

Results

Full deployment at NASA JSC's thermal vaccuum chamber with all sub-systems integrated!
  • At the end of my internship, LAMPS was successfully deployed at NASA JSC (where Apollo and JWST were tested!)
