Cold-Sprayed Rocket Nozzle Stress Modeling

FEA Simulation Results of a Rocket Nozzle (cross-section)

My senior Capstone design project was to design a proof-of-concept rocket nozzle for small-scale liquid-fuel rockets using cold-spray additive manufacturing (a solid-state deposition 3D printing process).


Evaluation of feasibility required conducting stress analysis of various nozzle designs and cooling channel geometries.


I led the effort to perform the analytical calculations, to re-design the nozzle and cooling channels, and to simulate the stress profile on the nozzle using finite element analysis (FEA).


Analytical Calculations

Setup for Analytical Calculations
  • Use thick-walled pressure vessel equations to calculate stress from combustion pressure on inlet (greatest stress) - both with and without pressure exerted by kerosene coolant
  • Utilize cold-sprayed copper tensile test results from team for material properties
  • Incorporate thermal softening data for copper at 500C (uses the aerospace industry standard for reduction of yield strength by 60%)
  • Create MATLAB script to determine minimum wall thickness at 500C

Finite Element Analysis Modeling in ABAQUS

  • Design nozzle and cooling channel geometry in SolidWorks
  • Incorporate design for manufacturing (DFM) findings from team (ex. Maximum overhang angle, minimum channel radius)
  • Perform finite element analysis simulation in ABAQUS standard: create mesh, enforce boundary conditions, and apply expected combustion loading and coolant channel pressure on nozzle
  • Conduct mesh refinement study on areas of maximum Von Mises stress and compare results to maximum allowable stress (incorporating thermal softening and factor of safety)

Comparison of FEA Results to Analytical Calculations

  • FEA simultation utilized the Von Mises stress criterion (calculates distortion energy, suitable for ductile materials)
  • Found exact dimensions on model from CAD and re-calculated Von Mises stress
  • Analytical calculation results aligned well with FEA simulation results

Results

  • Design was acceptable (max Von Mises stress < allowable stress)
  • Bend between inlet and and cooling channels were areas of stress concentrations - could be minimized in future designs
  • Analytical calculations for Von Mises stress aligned well with FEA simulation results, allowing for easier initial evaluation of designs prior to running FEA
