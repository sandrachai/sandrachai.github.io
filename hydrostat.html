Hydrostatic Testing of a Liquid-Fuel Rocket Engine

Hydrostatic Testing of Rocket Engine Graphite Gasket

AeroNU – Northeastern University’s aerospace club – is developing a rocket engine as a part of a challenge to build, test, and fly a liquid-fuel rocket.


Prior to hot fire testing of the rocket engine, the engine, injector, and all pipe sealing must be tested.


I led the testing effort to ensure proper sealing of the engine manifold, injector system, and horizontal test stand piping through a series of hydrostatic leak tests.


Testing

Hydrostatic testing of Injector Assembly with Graphite Gaskets
  • Test thread sealants (Gasolia, LOX8) at expected operating pressures
  • Test sealing of metal-filled graphite gaskets at both the engine and injector manifold to isolate cause of leaks
  • Verify necessary torques on JIC and NPT fittings and necessary pre-load on bolts

Process

Pipe Fitting Stack-Up for Injector
  • Create pipe fitting assemblies with avaliable fittings to connect parts (ex. Analog pressure gauge to engine to bike pump)
  • Apply thread sealants (for NPT) and crush washers (for JIC) fittings
  • Torque fittings to suggested spec
  • Fill assembly completely full with water (water is relatively incompressible = lower elastic energy - this is done for safety)
  • Pressurize system with bike pump until leaks (if any) are observed - identify pressure and location at which leaks occur
  • Diagnose location of leaks with soap bubble testing
  • Inspect for defects if leaks occur/ increase torque used

Example of Observed Leak at Gasket Interface Due to Insufficient Sealing

Results

Failure of Graphite Gasket at 1300 psi
  • Tested metal-filled graphite gasket to failure at 1300 psi on rocket engine
  • Determined necessary torques for bolts and fittings used
  • Validated LOX8 sealant (Gasolia becomes brittle at cryogenic temperatures)
  • Found a number of potential failure modes causing leaks (ex. foreign object debris (FOD) at interface of JIC crush washers, rusting of bolts from residual moisture)
